( About )

Building products,
not just features.

I'm Darshan — a senior Flutter developer and product-focused engineer based in India, with 5+ years of shipping real products.

I've spent the last five years in the intersection of product thinking and engineering execution. Most developers write code. I build products — from the initial idea discussion with a client to the final deployment on app stores and servers.

My primary tool is Flutter, and I've pushed it further than most — building applications that run natively on Android, iOS, Windows, and macOS from a single codebase. But my work goes well beyond the frontend. I design and build the Node.js backends, architect the databases, integrate payment systems, and even program IoT hardware when the product demands it.

What sets me apart isn't a technology — it's the approach. I think like a founder. When I work on a project, I care about the user experience as much as the code architecture. I ask "why are we building this?" before "how should we build this?" That mindset has led me to create products that genuinely work in the real world — not just in demo environments.

I've built community platforms used by thousands of families, restaurant systems that operate entirely offline with IoT integration, and enterprise tools deployed across four operating systems. Each project pushed me into new territory — payments, offline-first architecture, real-time systems, hardware communication — and I chose to learn deeply rather than delegate.

When I'm not building products, I'm researching. I experiment with new architectural patterns, explore edge cases in Flutter's rendering engine, and prototype ideas that might become the next project. The technology industry rewards curiosity, and I stay invested in understanding not just what works, but why it works.

01

Founder Mindset

I treat every project as if it's my own company. Product decisions aren't just someone else's problem — they're my responsibility. This leads to better products.

02

Business → Tech Bridge

I speak business fluently. I translate client needs into technical architecture without losing nuance. This eliminates the communication gap that kills most projects.

03

Full-Stack Ownership

Frontend, backend, database, deployment, IoT — I don't hand off to someone else. Owning the entire stack means fewer integration bugs and faster shipping.

04

R&D as a Habit

I dedicate time to experimentation. Whether it's Dart server internals, ESP32 communication protocols, or new state management patterns — I invest in understanding before implementing.

05

Offline-First Philosophy

Internet is a luxury, not a given. My systems are designed to work without connectivity — and sync gracefully when it returns. This isn't a feature, it's an architecture choice.

06

Ship, Then Iterate

Perfect is the enemy of live. I ship production-ready products, gather real feedback, and iterate. Every product I've built is actively used — not sitting in a portfolio.
