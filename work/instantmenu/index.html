InstantMenu

A digital QR menu and online ordering platform that helps restaurants go paperless and serve faster — from dine-in to delivery.

Platforms Android, Web

01 — The Problem

Small restaurants stuck on paper

Most small to mid-sized restaurants in India still operate with printed menus, manual order-taking, and fragmented ordering processes. During the post-COVID shift, many attempted digital solutions but found them either too complex or too expensive.

Existing platforms demanded high commissions, forced customers through cumbersome apps, and lacked essential features like thermal printer support — something every restaurant needs for kitchen operations.

02 — The Solution

Simple scan. Instant order.

InstantMenu gives any restaurant a digital QR-based menu in minutes. Customers scan, browse, and order — no app download required. For the restaurant owner, orders flow directly to the kitchen via thermal printer integration.

The platform supports three distinct modes — Dine-In, Takeaway, and Delivery — with multi-vendor payment processing so restaurants can get paid instantly without waiting on aggregator payouts.

03 — Key Features

Designed for real restaurant operations

Online Ordering & Tracking

Real-time order placement and status tracking — customers see their order progress from kitchen to table or delivery.

Multi-Vendor Payments

Each restaurant receives direct payments. No middleman delays. Support for UPI, cards, and digital wallets.

Thermal Printer Support

Direct integration with thermal printers — orders print automatically in the kitchen format restaurant staff already know.

Dine-In, Takeaway, Delivery

Three ordering modes from a single platform. Restaurants configure what they support — no feature bloat, just what works for their operation.

04 — System & Architecture

Lightweight but production-grade

The web ordering interface is built with EJS server-side rendering for performance — no heavy SPAs needed for a customer scanning a QR code. The admin panel and restaurant management app is a native Android application built in Java.

The Node.js backend handles multi-tenant restaurant data, order routing, payment webhooks, and printer communication. The architecture is designed to support thousands of concurrent restaurants without degrading performance.
